Efficacy Testing of Aapryl Manager Skill Forecast Methodology

📅 January 31, 2026 🌎 Equity & Fixed Income

Abstract

Identifying active managers that have a high probability of outperforming their peers and relevant market benchmarks has traditionally been challenging for asset owners.

The objective of this paper is to show that using a combination of the portfolio replication techniques and skill metrics developed by Xponance and Aapryl, one could identify products that are more likely to outperform their style. We tested the efficacy of 6 skill-based metrics to predict forward-looking skill of investment managers across multiple equity investment style peer groups as well as, for the first time, fixed income peer groups. This update extends our prior equity-only analysis to include nine fixed income categories, spanning core, core plus, high yield, global bonds, EM bonds, bank loans, MBS, US corporate, and short-term investment grade strategies. Key Definitions

  • Manager Product: An investment portfolio or strategy managed by a portfolio manager, team, process and investment infrastructure.
  • Static Clone: Long-term passive replication portfolio consisting of 100% allocation to various market indices including indices focused on: style, region, fundamental or risk factors, market cap, and economic sensitivity. Weights are set by Xponance's proprietary algorithm using the historical returns of product and indices.
  • Dynamic Clone: Short-term passive replication portfolio consisting of 100% allocation to various market indices. Weights are set by Xponance's proprietary algorithm using the trailing 36 months of product and index returns.
  • Manager Skill: Product performance minus Static Clone Performance, normalized for market environment and peer group.
  • Aapryl Score: A composite score (1–5, where 1 = most skilled) derived from six skill-based metrics spanning Total Excess Return, Stock Selection Return, and Style Timing Return, each measured for both Consistency and Edge.

Methodology Overview

Development of More Accurate Manager Benchmarks (Clones) Through Portfolio Replication

Manager performance varies over time, driven by factors that managers directly control, as well as factors outside of their control. Many manager processes begin with a belief system or investment philosophy that dictates which factors from a broad universe of securities are filtered for consideration. The aggregation of these factors can broadly be described as representative of the manager's investment style. From there, managers typically perform additional fundamental or technical analysis in order to determine the final sub-set of securities that will comprise their portfolio. However, this initial filtering of factors leads to either explicit or implicit factor exposures that can have a meaningful impact on manager performance that is separate from their selection skill.

Because the performance of factors is subject to market cycles, manager peer group rankings are also subject to such cycles. This cyclicality, combined with investors' conflation of the performance impact of a manager's factor exposures (i.e., investment style) with their selection alpha, often leads to disappointing results: manager rankings that are not persistent, and high-ranking first-quartile managers falling below their peer medians over the market cycle. Our portfolio replication techniques allow investors to identify key factor exposures and to disaggregate the effects of factors from skill.

In order to identify factors significant to each manager product, we implemented a regularized regression technique that performs both factor selection and beta regularization to enhance our portfolio replication model's prediction accuracy and interpretability. We further applied a constrained optimization technique requiring all factor exposures to sum to 100% and each exposure to fall within a range of 0%–100%. This renders each manager clone as a transparent, investable, long-only factor replication portfolio. The methodology now covers both equity and fixed income managers, with fixed income clones constructed from duration, spread, and credit-factor indices.

Static Clone

Long-term passive replication. Captures persistent factor exposures via historical full-period returns. Weights are constrained 0%–100%, summing to 100%.

Dynamic Clone

Short-term passive replication using trailing 36-month returns. Reflects recent shifts in style or factor exposure in real time.

Manager Skill

Product performance minus Static Clone Performance. Isolates true selection alpha from factor-driven returns, normalized by peer group.

Development and Analysis of Key Skill Metrics

The skill metrics are derived by dissecting the non-style portion of the return using 3 types of excess return, each with 2 skill measurements.

Excess Returns

  • Total Excess Return: Manager Skill = Product performance minus Static Clone Performance
  • Stock / Security Selection Return: Product performance minus Dynamic Clone Performance
  • Style Timing Return: Dynamic Clone Performance minus Static Clone Performance

Skill Measurements (Calculated for each of the 3 excess return series)

  • Consistency: Measure of the frequency of positive excess return. The measure begins with a standard batting average calculation and then adjusts for the length of the track record, market conditions and peer group performance. The result is a Z-score representing the manager's relative position among similar investment products, with 0 being average.
  • Edge: Measure of the relative magnitude of positive vs. negative excess returns. The measure begins with an Omega ratio1 (0% threshold) calculation, then adjusts for the length of the track record, market conditions and peer group performance. The result is a Z-score representing the manager's relative position among similar investment products, with 0 being average.

1 Omega Ratio can be summarized as the probability-weighted ratio of gains versus losses for a threshold return target. It is unique in that it does not assume returns will follow a set distribution, and large outliers have a much stronger impact than many other return statistics.

Methodology Testing

Product Universe

We examined long-only equity strategies in the Evestment Expanded Monthly Database that had at least 36 months of consecutive returns between July 31, 2001 and January 31, 2026, and reported gross returns in US dollars. For fixed income, we examined strategies from December 31, 2011 through January 31, 2023 across nine fixed income peer groups.

A product was not evaluated unless it had the requisite 36 months of history needed to build the clone portfolios and calculate the performance metrics. The final skill metrics we evaluated allow us to test a 3-year forward-looking return. To minimize survivorship bias, we included dead products in our analysis until their final month in the database and replaced any missing time periods with returns from the Static Clone of the product.

We categorized the testing universe into peer groups based on investment style, market cap focus, and region (for equity), and by duration and spread category (for fixed income). The total number of products used in the test is shown below in Table 1.

Testing Procedure

Time Horizon: Equity: July 31, 2001 to January 31, 2026 (forward-looking 3-year windows). Fixed Income: December 31, 2011 to January 31, 2023.

Objective: To identify a group of managers that maximizes the forward-looking Manager Skill relative to their peer average, using independent skill variables as screening criteria.

Independent Variables: We evaluated all quarter-end observations for Total Consistency, Total Edge, Stock/Security Selection Consistency, Stock/Security Selection Edge, Style Edge, and Style Consistency. The composite Aapryl Score (1 = most skilled, 5 = least skilled) aggregates these six metrics.

Metrics: The optimization set a single fixed threshold for each variable. A product was identified as 'Skilled' (Aapryl Score = 1) if the scores for ALL variables were above the thresholds set.

Method: We used fixed thresholds from the optimization to test each product by quarter. Two key output metrics were computed: (1) the percentage-point increase in probability of finishing in the top quintile for Skilled vs. the peer-weighted average, and (2) the forward 36-month excess return of Skilled managers vs. the peer-weighted average (measured relative to each manager's static clone).

Conclusions and Next Steps

It is our belief that if there is true signal in a metric, it should not take a complex model to illustrate the value. Therefore, this test was designed to be simple and straightforward.

The primary goal of this updated analysis was to show that using the portfolio replication and skill metrics developed by Xponance and Aapryl, one could identify products that are likely to outperform their style, now across both equity and fixed income peer groups. The results support this thesis across a majority of the peer groups tested:

Equity 16 of 27 Peer Groups

16 of 27 equity peer groups showed positive excess alpha for Aapryl Score=1 managers. Global and International equity categories showed the most consistent outperformance, while certain US Large Cap and Mid Cap categories showed more muted results.

Fixed Income 13 of 13 Sub-Categories

13 of 13 fixed income sub-categories showed positive excess alpha for Aapryl Score=1 managers. MBS, EM Bonds, and Global Bonds demonstrated particularly strong signals. High Yield and Bank Loans showed more modest but still positive results on an alpha basis.

Top Quintile Likelihood: Across both asset classes, Aapryl Score=1 managers showed improved probability of finishing in the top quintile of forward performance in the majority of categories, validating the predictive power of the skill metrics.

Current Research Directions

While our results are strong, we continue to advance our research methodology to improve our model. We are currently developing and testing additional metrics and methodologies, including:

  • 2

  • 3
    Machine Learning: To identify predictors of negative skill and refine positive skill predictors
  • 4
    Expanded Fixed Income Coverage: Including Short Duration, TIPS, and Municipal Bond categories
  • 5
    Cross-Asset Analysis: Skill persistence analysis and multi-manager portfolio construction applications

As we gain confidence in these enhancements and incorporate them into Aapryl, we will provide updated efficacy tests so that you can share our confidence.
